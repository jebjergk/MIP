1) What MIP Is

MIP is an evidence-first decision system for research and controlled paper-trading workflows.

Analogy: MIP behaves like an air-traffic control tower. Signals are incoming flights, trust/risk are runway checks, and actions move only after full clearance.

2) How MIP Learns From Evidence

Data -> Signals -> Outcome Evaluation -> Training Metrics -> Trust -> Proposals -> Decisions -> Execution Flow -> Review

Signals are observations. Decisions are governed by policy and operational checks. Learning Ledger connects decisions to realized outcomes for tuning.

4) What Is Newly Implemented

  • News Intelligence can influence ranking/confidence and block entries under configured policy conditions.
  • Live-linked workflow has explicit lifecycle states; approved actions can still be blocked later by validation/freshness/approvals.
  • Reason codes and revalidation outcomes are now first-class diagnostics.
  • Learning Ledger supports recurring motif analysis for policy tuning.
Important: SUCCESS_WITH_SKIPS may be expected in no-data windows. Confirm step detail in Runs before treating as an incident.

5) Daily Operating Playbook

  1. Home: verify freshness.
  2. Cockpit: check what changed.
  3. Training Status: validate evidence quality.
  4. AI Agent Decisions: inspect verdicts/reason codes.
  5. Live Portfolio Activity: inspect lifecycle transitions.
  6. Runs: verify step-level diagnostics.

6) Worked Scenarios

Scenario A: Approved but not executed

  1. Check decision row and reason codes.
  2. Check lifecycle block in Live Portfolio Activity.
  3. Correlate with same run window in Runs.

Scenario B: HOT news but no action

  1. Check News Intelligence impact evidence.
  2. Confirm reason-code pattern in AI Agent Decisions.
  3. Validate policy/freshness state in activity feed.

Appendix B: FAQ

Why was an action approved but not executed?

Approval is not the final stage. Revalidation, freshness, and operational gates can still block progression.

Does HOT mean immediate trade execution?

No. HOT indicates decision-relevant context, not automatic gate bypass.
